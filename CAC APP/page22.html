Interest - Types

There are two types of interest:

Simple Interest:

Simple interest is like getting a small bonus on your money. Let's say you saved $10 in your bank, and the bank gives you 5% interest. That means the bank will add 5% of $10 to your money. 5% of $10 is 50 cents, so you'll have $10 + 50 cents = $10.50 after a year.

Compound Interest:

Let's use the same example. You have $10 in your bank, and the bank gives you 5% compound interest. In the beginning, you'll get 5% of $10, just like with simple interest (50 cents). But here's the magic: the next time, the bank gives you 5% of $10.50, which is 53 cents. It's like interest on top of interest!

Did you know: Savings account rates are primarily impacted by Federal Reserve policy.
